My Educational Philosophy

"Teachers open the door, but you must enter yourself."

I believe students everywhere have the potential to grow into inquiring, well-rounded global citizens through constructivist learning experiences. The instructional resources I create reflect the digital and communication demands our future generations will encounter.

I have seen my young students and adult learners perform best when engaged in activities that build curiosity, empathy and critical thinking skills. Inquiry-based learning allows students to drive their instruction while facilitated by instructors who provide necessary resources and opportunities.

I work to ensure students have agency in their learning and teachers feel supported to make necessary changes. When faced with a future heavily influenced by technology and global interaction, students need learning experiences that prepare them with digital literacy and diversity skills.

Instruction as a Solution

As a Master of Instructional Technology student at UNC Wilmington, our team identified a need in Charlotte-Mecklenburg Schools regarding the use of Promethean ActivPanels. We conducted a thorough front-end analysis and found teachers weren't utilizing the technology to its fullest potential.

We recommended a "train the trainer" professional development program, allowing school leadership to learn and then teach other personnel. This solution prepared us for client-facing roles in education technology.
