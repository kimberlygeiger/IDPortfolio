Welcome!

My name is Kimberly Baker, and I am an instructional designer. I love it when students love to learn, and teachers love to teach. I'm here to make it happen.

Let's chat

Student facing materials & content

Students require highly engaging, relevant and intentional resources. My work ensures that students are interested and learning throughout each lesson. I write diverse content for many subjects across K-12 and promote global citizenship and inquiry-based learning wherever I can.

Teacher resources

Teachers rely on ready-to-go, standards-driven resources to supplement their instruction. Curriculum materials that are rooted in academic standards and allow for flexibility are my specialty.

professional development

My mentorship experience and online coursework helps educators around the country recognize their potential in the classroom. I relate to educators as learners and seek to make each learning experience impactful and intentional.

PEDAGOGY

"Teachers open the door, but you must enter yourself."

I believe students everywhere have the potential to grow into inquiring, well-rounded global citizens through constructivist learning experiences. The instructional resources I include in my work reflect the digital and communication demands our future generations will encounter as they grow. As a teacher, I have seen my young students and adult learners perform best when they are engaged in activities that build curiosity, empathy and critical thinking skills. Inquiry-based learning allows students to drive their instruction in a way that best fits their needs, while facilitated by an instructor who provides them with necessary resources and opportunities. I work to ensure that students have agency in their learning and teachers feel supported and able to make changes when necessary when working with my curriculum. When faced with a future that is so heavily influenced by technology and interaction across the globe, students should have learning experiences that prepare them with digital literacy and diversity skills. My goal is to provide this opportunity for as many classrooms as possible.
